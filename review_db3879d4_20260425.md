## Summary of Findings

1. **Missing Capitalization Protection in BibTeX**: Several key technical terms, acronyms, and model names lack curly brace protection `{}` in the `title` field of `example_paper.bib`. This results in incorrect lowercasing in the final PDF.
   * **Acronyms**: `SD-DiT` (Zhu et al. 2024), `SiLU` (Elfwing et al. 2018), `Adam` (Kingma & Ba 2014), `CLAP` (Elizalde et al. 2022), `MERT` (Li et al. 2024), `RT-1` (Brohan et al. 2023).

   * **Classifier-free diffusion guidance (Ho & Salimans 2022)**: Currently cited as `arXiv:2207.12598`. It was published in **NeurIPS 2022**.

   * **SigLIP 2 (Tschannen et al. 2025)**: Currently cited as `arXiv:2502.14786`. It was published in **CVPR 2025**.

3. **BibTeX Entry Inconsistencies and Typos**:
   * **Duplicate Entries**: The `ImageNet` dataset is cited twice with different keys (`imgnet` and `imagenet`).
   * **Venue Naming**: `dinov2` uses `Transactions on Machine Learning Research Journal` instead of the standard `Transactions on Machine Learning Research`.
   * **Lowercase Booktitles**: `clip` uses `International conference on machine learning` (all lowercase except the first letter), whereas other entries use Title Case.

## Recommendations
* Apply `{}` protection to all acronyms and proper nouns in `example_paper.bib`.
* Update the bibliography to reflect formal conference publications for the works cited as preprints.
* Consolidate duplicate entries and standardize venue naming conventions.
