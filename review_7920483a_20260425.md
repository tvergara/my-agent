# Bibliography Audit for Paper 7920483a

## Overview
I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliography (extracted from `draft_zongyu.bbl`) and LaTeX source for the paper "Compression as Adaptation: Implicit Visual Representation with Diffusion Foundation Models" (ID: 7920483a-697c-4733-bafd-f3810bf9df0a). The audit focused on reference formatting, acronym protection, and citation currency.

## Key Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several papers are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been published in major peer-reviewed venues:
- **Stochastic Interpolants** (`albergo2023stochastic`) was published in **NeurIPS 2023**.
- **Diffusion Posterior Sampling** (`chung2022diffusion`) was published in **ICLR 2023**.
- **From Explicit CoT to Implicit CoT** (`deng2024explicit`) was published in **EMNLP 2024**.

### 2. Missing Acronym Protection in Titles
Several entries fail to protect acronyms and proper nouns with curly braces `{}`. This leads to incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles:
- **LoRA** (e.g., in "Lora: Low-rank adaptation of large language models")
- **HEVC** (e.g., in "Hevc offical test model")
- **COIN** (e.g., in "COIN: COmpression with implicit neural representations")
- **INR** (Implicit Neural Representations - should be protected if appearing as an acronym)
- **Gaussian** (in "discretized Gaussian mixture likelihoods")

### 3. Incomplete Metadata
The reference `flynn16common` appears to have incomplete venue information: "Joint Collaborative Team Video Coding ITU-T SG, 16". Adding more specific volume/issue or meeting details would improve traceability.

## Recommendation
I recommend that the authors:
1. Update the `.bib` file (from which the `.bbl` was generated) to include curly brace protection for all acronyms (LoRA, HEVC, COIN, etc.) and proper nouns (Gaussian).
2. Replace arXiv entries with their corresponding peer-reviewed publication details (NeurIPS, ICLR, EMNLP).
3. Ensure consistent and complete metadata for all technical reports and standard documents.
