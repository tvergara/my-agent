# Bibliographic Audit for Multi-Agent RL (c993ba35)

I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliographic references in `main.bib` for the submission "Learning Approximate Nash Equilibria in Cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning via Mean-Field Subsampling".

**Paper ID:** c993ba35-65e0-4290-a66a-c128e33410f4

## Key Findings

### 1. Missing Capitalization Protection
Technical acronyms and model names are not consistently protected with curly braces, which leads to incorrect rendering in many BibTeX styles:
- **`lazarsfeld2025fastfurioussymmetriclearning`**: `Fast and Furious Symmetric Learning` should be `{F}ast and {F}urious {S}ymmetric {L}earning`.
- **`aina2025deepreinforcementlearningmultiagent`**: `Deep Reinforcement Learning` is partially protected as `{R}einforcement {L}earning`.
- **`Silver_Huang_Maddison_Guez_Sifre_van_den_Driessche_Schrittwieser_Antonoglou_Panneershelvam_Lanctot_et_al._2016`**: `Go` should be protected as `{G}o`.
- **`shalevshwartz2016safemultiagentreinforcementlearning`**: `Safe, Multi-Agent, Reinforcement Learning` should be protected.
- **`9570295`**: `Q-Learning` should be protected.

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several works are cited as preprints despite having been formally published:
- **`lin2022online`**: Cited as `arXiv preprint arXiv:2210.12320`.
- **`leonardos2025globalconvergencemultiagentpolicy`**: Cited as `eprint={2106.01969}`.
- **`anand2024efficientreinforcementlearningglobal`**: Cited as `eprint={2403.00222}`.

### 3. Duplicate and Inconsistent Entries
- **Duplicates**: `Sutton_McAllester_Singh_Mansour_1999` and `NIPS1999_464d828b` are duplicate entries for the same "Policy Gradient Methods" paper.
- **Inconsistent Venues**:
  - `ding2022independent`: `booktitle={International Conference on Machine Learning}`.
  - `fujimoto2019off`: `booktitle={International conference on machine learning}` (lowercase "conference").
  - `Sutton_McAllester_Singh_Mansour_1999`: `booktitle={Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems}`.
  - `NIPS1999_464d828b`: `booktitle={Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems}`.

### 4. Author Name Formatting
The formatting of author names is inconsistent across the file:
- **`lin2022online`**: `author={Lin, Yiheng and Preiss, James A. and ...}` (Last, First).
- **`lazarsfeld2025fastfurioussymmetriclearning`**: `author={John Lazarsfeld and Georgios Piliouras and ...}` (First Last).
Standardizing to one format (preferably "Last, First") is recommended for professional presentation.

## Recommendations
*   Standardize author name formats across all entries.
*   Apply curly braces `{}` to all acronyms, proper nouns, and model names in titles.
*   Update arXiv preprints to their final peer-reviewed versions.
*   Remove duplicate entries (e.g., the Sutton et al. 1999 paper).
*   Ensure consistent capitalization and naming for major conferences like ICML and NeurIPS.
