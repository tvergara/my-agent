# Citation Formatting Audit for Paper c993ba35

**Paper ID:** c993ba35-65e0-4290-a66a-c128e33410f4
**Audit Date:** 2026-04-25

## Summary
The bibliographic sources for this paper exhibit significant inconsistencies in naming conventions, metadata accuracy, and structural organization.

## Key Findings

### 1. Messy and Non-Standard Citation Keys
A significant number of citation keys do not follow standard academic naming conventions (e.g., `AuthorYear` or `Author_Year`):
- **DOI-based keys**: `doi:10.1137/1.9781611976465.84`, `doi:10.1137/17M1139461`.
- **UUID-based keys**: `409cf137-dbb5-3eb1-8cfe-0743c3dc925f`.
- **Special characters**: `Kakade+Langford:2002` (contains `+` and `:`).
- **Inconsistent separators**: Keys vary between `AuthorYear` (`Banach1922`), `Author_Year` (`Daskalakis_2014`), and `authorYear` (`lin2022online`).

### 2. Inconsistent Metadata and Venue Naming
- **Capitalization Discrepancies**: The same venue is cited with different casing across entries, e.g., "International Conference on Machine Learning" (`ding2022independent`) vs "International conference on machine learning" (`fujimoto2019off`).
- **Trailing Spaces**: Several entries contain leading or trailing spaces in critical fields like `title` (e.g., `pmlr-v151-chen22i`).

### 3. Metadata Accuracy and Outdated Entries
- **Year Mismatch**: `leonardos2025globalconvergencemultiagentpolicy` is listed with `year={2025}` but refers to a 2021 preprint (`eprint={2106.01969}`).
- **Formally Published Preprints**: Entries like `lin2022online` and `chen2021lyapunov` are listed as arXiv preprints from several years ago and may have been formally published since.

### 4. Acronym Protection
There is inconsistent use of curly braces `{}` to protect acronyms and proper nouns in titles. While some entries use them (e.g., `Independent policy gradient... Markov potential games`), others lack protection for terms like `TD-learning`, `UCFH`, `MDP`, and `RL`, which will likely result in incorrect lowercasing.

## Conclusion
The bibliography requires substantial cleanup to achieve professional academic quality. Standardizing citation keys, unifying venue names, and ensuring proper acronym protection are essential steps to improve the manuscript's presentation.
