### Reasoning for Citation Audit - Paper d50ca57f

**Paper ID:** d50ca57f-ac9a-438f-b0f5-fab02c8d64df
**Title:** Transport Clustering: Solving Low-Rank Optimal Transport via Clustering

#### Audit Findings

I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliography (`references.bib`) for this submission. My review identified several issues that should be addressed to ensure academic rigor and consistent formatting:

1. **Missing Capitalization Protection for Acronyms**:
    - Technical acronyms in title fields are missing curly brace `{}` protection. Standard BibTeX styles will lowercase these terms if not protected.
    - Examples:
        - `ODE` in `title={How to train your neural ODE: the world of Jacobian and kinetic regularization}`
        - `k-Median` in `title = {A Nearly Linear-Time Approximation Scheme for the Euclidean k-Median Problem}`
        - `EMD` (implied in `earth mover distance`) and others.

2. **Inconsistent Conference Naming**:
    - There is a lack of standardization in `booktitle` fields. Some entries use highly abbreviated names while others use full titles.
    - Examples:
        - `booktitle = {ICML}` and `booktitle = {NeurIPS}` should be expanded to their full names (e.g., *Proceedings of the International Conference on Machine Learning*) or at least protected as `{ICML}`.
        - `journal={ICLR}` is used for the *International Conference on Learning Representations*, which should be listed under `booktitle`.
        - `booktitle = {2009 IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition(CVPR)}` mixes full name and abbreviation in a different format than other entries.

3. **Inconsistent "k-means" vs "K-means"**:
    - The bibliography uses both `k-means` (e.g., `title={Kernel k-means: spectral clustering and normalized cuts}`) and `K-means` inconsistently. While both are used in literature, consistency within a single bibliography is preferred.

#### Conclusion
Updating these references to include proper case protection and standardized conference names will improve the overall professional appearance and bibliographic rigor of the manuscript.
