# Bibliography Audit - Paper 230fcebb

## Overview
I have audited the bibliography of the paper "Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View" (ID: 230fcebb-7586-46e3-9897-191540be9efa). The audit identified issues with redundant definitions, acronym protection, and venue consistency.

## Findings

### 1. Extensive Redundant String Definitions
The `.bib` file contains two near-identical sets of `@STRING` definitions (lines 2-21 and 61-80, as well as lines 25 and 84, and lines 30-36 and 89-95).
- Redundant definitions for `PAMI`, `ACL`, `EMNLP`, `ICLR`, `ICML`, etc., increase the risk of maintenance errors.
- Conflicting definitions for the same venue (e.g., `confNIPS` using "NIPS" vs `confNEU` using "NeurIPS") can lead to inconsistent naming conventions in the final references.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection for Acronyms and Proper Nouns
Several entries lack curly brace `{}` protection for technical terms in their titles or metadata:
- **Acronyms**: `CDE` (walker2025structured), `IBM` (Samuel59).
- **Proper Nouns**: `Kimi` (team2025kimi).
- **Locations**: `MA` (mitchell80).
Without protection, these terms may be incorrectly lowercased (e.g., "kimi" instead of "Kimi") in styles like `unsrt`.

### 3. Inconsistent Venue Capitalization
Venue names are inconsistently capitalized across different entries:
- `hafner2019learning`: "International conference on machine learning" (Sentence case).
- `morrill2021neural`: "International Conference on Machine Learning" (Title case).
- `sieber2024understanding`: "Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems" (Title case).

### 4. Outdated arXiv Citations
Multiple papers are cited as preprints despite having been available for several years or having likely formal versions:
- `beckett2022symplectic`: arXiv:2203.07405 (2022).
- `chevyrev2024multiplicative`: arXiv:2406.16856 (2024).
- `chen2022transdreamer`: arXiv:2202.09481 (2022).
- `walker2024log`: arXiv:2402.18512 (2024).

## Recommendation
Consolidate the `@STRING` definitions into a single, canonical set. Add curly brace protection to all acronyms and proper nouns. Standardize conference naming to use Title Case consistently. Update arXiv preprints to their formal conference or journal counterparts where available.
