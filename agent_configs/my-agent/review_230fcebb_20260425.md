# Bibliography and Citation Audit - Paper 230fcebb

## Paper Information
- **Title**: Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View
- **ID**: 230fcebb-7586-46e3-9897-191540be9efa

## Audit Findings

### 1. Missing Acronym and Proper Noun Protection (BibTeX)
The ICML bibliography style (`icml2026.bst`) enforces sentence case for titles (`{ title "t" change.case$ }`). Many entries lack curly brace `{}` protection for acronyms and proper names, leading to incorrect lowercasing.

- **Lie Algebras:** Crucially for this paper, the name "Lie" is unprotected in many entries (e.g., `agrachev2013control`, `hall2013lie`, `jurdjevic1972control`). These will render as "lie" (the verb) instead of "Lie" (the mathematician's name).
- **Architectural Acronyms:** xLSTM, GLA, RNN, Transformer, BERT, LSTM, SSM, TFLA, and CNN are frequently unprotected (e.g., `beck2024xlstm`, `yang2024gated`, `gers2001lstm`).

### 2. LaTeX Syntax Errors and Typographical Issues
- **Typo in `coffi2007produit`:** The title contains `alg$\backslash$ebres de Lie`. The sequence `$\backslash$ebres` is a malformed attempt at `alg├©bres`.
- **Casing in `vaswani2017attention`:** The title "Attention is All you Need" should ideally be "Attention is All You Need".

### 3. Outdated Citations
Several entries are cited as preprints but have formal conference publications:
- `beck2024xlstm`: Cited as arXiv 2024; should be updated to **NeurIPS 2024**.
- `yang2024gated`: Cited as arXiv 2024; should be updated to **ICML 2024**.

## Evidence
- `icml2026.bst:309`: Enforced sentence case for titles.
- `main.bib:107`: Malformed LaTeX sequence.
- Source audit of `main.bib` and `main_2.bib`.

## Conclusion
A comprehensive sweep of the bibliography is recommended to update formal citations and protect all technical acronyms and the proper name "Lie" to ensure professional and accurate formatting.
