# Bibliography and Citation Audit: Parallelizable Sequence Models (230fcebb)

## Summary
I have audited the bibliography and citation formatting for "Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View". The paper demonstrates high technical quality, but the bibliographic records and in-text citations require standardization to meet ICML submission guidelines.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Inconsistent Citation Commands
The paper uses `\cite` for many citations that should semantically be `\citep`.
- **Misuse**: I found 21 uses of `\cite`. Many occur at the end of sentences or in parenthetical positions where `\citep` is the standard for the ICML author-year style.
- **Examples**:
    - Line 292: `reproduce another's behavior \cite{liu2022transformers}.` (Should be `\citep`)
    - Line 534: `brittleness to generalization \cite{liu2022transformers},` (Should be `\citep`)

### 2. Bibliography Capitalization Protection
The `references.bib` and `main_2.bib` files lack curly brace `{}` protection for several proper nouns and technical terms that will be incorrectly lowercased by the ICML bibliography style.
- **Proper Nouns**: `Lie` (as in Lie Algebra) is unprotected in multiple titles (e.g., `Chapter 18 Extensions of Lie algebras`).
- **Acronyms and Terms**: `Transformer`, `State-Space Models`, and `RNN` are frequently unprotected in titles, leading to rendering like "transformer" or "state-space models".

### 3. Bibliography Duplication and Redundancy
- **Duplicate Entries**: There is significant overlap between `main.bib` and `main_2.bib`. Several entries (e.g., `DudaHart2nd`, `kearns89`, `MachineLearningI`, `mitchell80`, `Newell81`, `Samuel59`) are duplicated across both files. This can lead to conflicts or inconsistent metadata during the BibTeX compilation process.
- **Mixed Macros**: String definitions (e.g., `confICLR`) are mixed with entry data, which is valid but contributes to the lack of organization in the bibliography files.

## Recommendations
- Replace `\cite` with `\citep` for all parenthetical citations.
- Unify `main.bib` and `main_2.bib` into a single, de-duplicated bibliography file.
- Apply curly brace protection to all occurrences of `Lie`, `Transformer`, `SSM`, `RNN`, `LSTM`, and other technical proper nouns in titles.
