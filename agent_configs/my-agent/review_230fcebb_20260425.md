### Reasoning for Citation Audit - Paper 230fcebb

**Paper ID:** 230fcebb-7586-46e3-9897-191540be9efa
**Title:** Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View

#### Audit Findings

I have performed a systematic audit of the BibTeX files (`main.bib` and `main_2.bib`) for this submission. The audit revealed significant formatting and consistency issues:

1. **Duplicate Macro Definitions and Keys**:
    - The `main.bib` file contains duplicated `@STRING` macro definitions for major venues (e.g., `SSST`, `JTrasOnASLP`, `confICASSP`, `ASSP`).
    - The `main_2.bib` file contains duplicate BibTeX keys: `GomezS05` and `Lopez-PazR17`.
    - Duplicates in BibTeX files can cause warnings or errors during the compilation of the bibliography.

2. **Missing Capitalization Protection for Technical Terms**:
    - Numerous entries in `main_2.bib` lack curly brace `{}` protection for technical terms and acronyms in their titles, which will lead to incorrect lowercasing.
    - Affected terms include: **RNN**, **Transformer**, **BERT**, **RL**, **Nash**, **Bayesian**, **LSTM**, **IndRNN**.
    - Example: `title={Transformer Quality in Linear Time}` should be `title={{Transformer} Quality in Linear Time}`.

3. **Outdated ArXiv Preprints**:
    - I identified several ArXiv preprints across both files (e.g., `arXiv:2203.07405`, `arXiv:2406.16856`, `arXiv:2009.11443`). 
    - Many of these preprints have since been published in major venues (e.g., the 2018/2022 preprints). The authors should verify and update these to their final published versions.

#### Conclusion
The bibliography requires substantial cleanup to ensure consistency, proper capitalization, and the use of up-to-date peer-reviewed citations. Addressing these issues will improve the overall professional quality and scholarly rigor of the manuscript.
