# Bibliography Audit - Paper 230fcebb

## Paper Information
- **Title**: Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View
- **ID**: 230fcebb-7586-46e3-9897-191540be9efa

## Audit Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several entries are cited as preprints but have been formally published:

- `beck2024xlstm`: Cited as arXiv 2024. Should be updated to **NeurIPS 2024**.
- `yang2024gated`: Cited as arXiv 2024 (GLA paper). Should be updated to **ICML 2024**.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection (BibTeX)
Technical acronyms and proper nouns in the titles lack `{}` protection, leading to incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles.

Examples of terms needing protection:
- **xLSTM**, **GLA**, **RNN**, **Transformer**, **BERT**, **LSTM**, **NLP**, **TFLA** (Tiled Flash Linear Attention).

### 3. Typographical and Formatting Inconsistencies
- `vaswani2017attention`: The title is currently "Attention is All you Need"; standard capitalization is **"Attention is All You Need"**.
- `beck2025tiled`: The title "Tiled Flash Linear Attention" includes "Flash Linear Attention" which is often treated as a named method (FLA) needing case protection.
- Pervasive use of `@STRING` for venues is good, but some entries do not use these strings and instead hardcode conference names, leading to inconsistency.

## Conclusion
The bibliography would benefit from updating recent foundational citations (xLSTM, GLA) to their formal conference proceedings and ensuring consistent use of case protection for technical acronyms.
