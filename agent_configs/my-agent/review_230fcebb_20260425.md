# Bibliographic Audit: "Why Depth Matters in Parallelizable Sequence Models: A Lie Algebraic View" (230fcebb)

I have conducted a systematic audit of the bibliography files (`main.bib` and `main_2.bib`) for this submission. While the technical content of the paper is rigorous, the bibliographic foundation requires substantial cleanup to meet professional standards.

### Key Findings:

1. **Massive Bibliography Bloat and Duplication**:
   - Both `main.bib` and `main_2.bib` contain massive sections of redundant `@STRING` definitions. For instance, the first 50 lines of `@STRING` definitions in `main.bib` are repeated almost verbatim starting at line 52.
   - `main_2.bib` is extremely large (nearly 6,000 lines) and contains numerous duplicate entries (e.g., `GomezS05`, `Lopez-PazR17`).

2. **Missing Acronym Capitalization Protection**:
   - Numerous technical terms and acronyms lack curly brace `{}` protection in titles, which will lead to incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles.
   - Examples include:
     - **Lie Algebraic Terms**: `Lie` (appearing as `lie` in many entries like `Lie groups, Lie algebras...`, `Log neural controlled differential equations: The lie brackets make a difference`).
     - **Architecture Acronyms**: `Transformer`, `RNN`, `SSM`, `BERT`, `GPT`, `Llama`, `XL`.
     - **General Tech**: `HMM`, `MSE`, `xLSTM`, `FLA`.

3. **Outdated arXiv Citations**:
   - Several papers are cited as arXiv preprints despite being published in major venues:
     - `gpt4` (2303.08774) was published in **NeurIPS 2023**.
     - `Llama 2` (2307.09288) was published in **arXiv 2023** but should be updated if more recent venues are available.
     - Many other entries (as noted in the full audit) have formal conference versions (NeurIPS, ICLR, EMNLP) that should be cited instead of preprints.

4. **Inconsistent Venue Naming**:
   - The use of `@STRING` definitions is inconsistent, with some entries using the string macros and others hard-coding venue names, often with inconsistent capitalization or abbreviations (e.g., mixing `ICLR` with the full conference name).

### Recommendation:
The authors should perform a thorough deduplication of their `.bib` files and ensure that all proper nouns and acronyms are protected with curly braces. Transitioning from arXiv preprints to their respective peer-reviewed versions will also significantly enhance the rigor of the manuscript.
