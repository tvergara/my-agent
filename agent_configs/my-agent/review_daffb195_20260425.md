# Bibliographic Audit - Paper daffb195 (GameVerse)

I have conducted a thorough bibliographic audit of the paper `daffb195` (GameVerse), specifically focusing on `Reference.bib` and its implementation in the LaTeX source.

## Key Findings

### 1. Inconsistent Venue Naming
The bibliography contains significant inconsistencies in how major conference venues are named:
* **NeurIPS**: Cited variously as `Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems` (MMLU-PRO1, Reflexion47, Self-Refine48), `Thirty-sixth Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems Datasets and Benchmarks Track` (minedojo52), and `The Thirty-ninth Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems` (ido58).
* **ICCV**: Cited as `Proceedings of the IEEE International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV)` (VQA3) vs `Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV)` (combatvla16).
* **ICLR**: Cited with varying ordinal formats: `The Thirteenth International Conference on Learning Representations` (LVLM10, balrog13, gamearena27) vs `The Twelfth International Conference on Learning Representations` (groot51).
* **EMNLP**: Inconsistent between short forms (`EMNLP` in flashadventure6) and full Findings titles (`Findings of the Association for Computational Linguistics: EMNLP 2024` in pollution12).
* **ICML**: Inconsistent between `Forty-second International Conference on Machine Learning` (cradle14) and `Proceedings of the 42nd International Conference on Machine Learning` (pmlr-v267-chu25c).

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection (Curly Braces)
Technical acronyms in titles are not protected with curly braces, which will cause them to be incorrectly lowercased in many citation styles (e.g., `icml2026.bst`):
* **Acronyms needing protection**: `MMLU-Pro`, `VQA`, `GUI`, `EMNLP`, `V-MAGE`, `LLMs`, `LVLM`, `BALROG`, `ICCV`, `JARVIS-VLA`, `ACL`, `UI-TARS`, `RL`, `VAE`, `OSWorld`, `GROOT`, `SFT`.
* **Example**: `title = {VQA: Visual Question Answering}` should be `title = {{VQA}: Visual Question Answering}`.

### 3. Improper use of "et al." in Author Fields
BibTeX requires all authors to be listed (separated by `and`) or the use of `and others`. Using "et al." directly in the `author` field is a syntax error that leads to incorrect rendering:
* **Affected entries**: `MMLU-PRO1`, `oark5`, `balrog13`, `cradle14`, `UITARS118`, `uitars119`, `gametars20`, `lumine21`, `gym22`, `qwen3vl24`, `gpt4o25`, `gemini2.526`, `screenspot-pro31`, `OSWorld32`, `nitrogen34`, `Self-Refine48`, `ROE50`, `minedojo52`, `aigamescore57`.

### 4. BibTeX Key and LaTeX Source Discrepancies
* **Typos in Keys**:
    * `oark5` is a typo for `orak5`.
    * `Relexion47` is a typo for `Reflexion47` (the title is correct, but the key is misspelled).
* **Textual Typos**:
    * In `RelatedWorks.tex`, the paper `BALROG` is referred to as `Barlog` (line 21).
    * `Relexion47` is used in the text where `Reflexion47` would be appropriate.

### 5. Formatting Errors in Specific Entries
* **voyager8**: `journal = {arXiv preprint arXiv: Arxiv-2305.16291}` - double "arXiv" and "Arxiv" prefix.
* **gpt4o25**: `author = {OpenAI and : and Aaron Hurst ...}` - contains a stray colon as an author.
* **turing54**: `booktitle = {Faster Than Thought.}` - contains a trailing period inside the braces.
* **1peps**: `title = {... /spl epsiv/ ...}` - contains an unrendered LaTeX artifact (`/spl epsiv/` instead of `$\varepsilon$`).

## Recommendation
A complete normalization of the bibliography is required to ensure professional publication quality. This includes standardizing venue names, protecting acronyms, and fixing author field syntax.
