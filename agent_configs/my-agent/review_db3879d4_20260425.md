# Bibliographic Audit - Paper db3879d4

**Paper Title:** Self-Supervised Flow Matching for Scalable Multi-Modal Synthesis
**Audit Date:** 2026-04-25

## Findings

I have performed a thorough review of the bibliography (`example_paper.bib`) for this submission. The reference list contains a significant number of technical issues that require attention to meet professional academic standards:

### 1. Duplicate BibTeX Entries
I identified redundant entries for the same works, which should be consolidated:
*   **"ImageNet..."**: Represented by both `imagenet` and `imgnet`.

### 2. Pervasive Missing Capitalization Protection for Acronyms and Model Names
There is a massive lack of curly brace `{}` protection for technical acronyms and specific model names in titles. This will result in these terms being incorrectly rendered in lowercase due to ICML sentence-case enforcement. Examples include:
*   **Model Names**: `DINOv2`, `DINOv3`, `REPA`, `REPA-E`, `Depth Anything 3`, `SiT`, `SongBloom`, `MAGVIT-v2`, `MaskDiT`, `FasterDiT`, `MDT`, `LDiT`, `VQ-VAE`, `VQ-GAN`, `ResNet`, `ViT`, `SimCLR`, `SDXL`, `TiTok`, `LangTiTok`, `TaTiTok`, `SD3`, `U-Net`, `DDPM`, `DDIM`, `PixArt-alpha`, `VDT`, `Lumina-T2X`, `FLUX`, `EQ-VAE`, `I-JEPA`, `FeatSharp`, `VideoREPA`, `JanusFlow`, `GenTron`, `U-ViT`, `SODA`, `GigaGAN`, `ARDiT`, `DART`, `SRA`, `RAE`, `DeCo`, `DDT`, `PixNerd`, `PixelFlow`, `JIT`, `VideoJAM`, `HuBERT`, `VTP`, `BEiT`, `SigLIP`, `VideoMAE`, `V-JEPA`, `VideoVLA`, `WorldVLA`.
*   **Technical Terms**: `GANs`, `AE`, `FID`, `sFID`, `IS`, `CKA`, `LPIPS`, `R-CNN`, `Fast R-CNN`, `Faster R-CNN`, `SIFT`, `VGG`, `SURF`, `ORB`.

### 3. Outdated Publication Venues
An unusually large number of works are cited as preprints despite having been formally published in major venues:
*   **"Adam: A method for stochastic optimization"** (`adam`): Should be updated to **ICLR 2015**.
*   **"Decoupled weight decay regularization"** (`adamw`): Should be updated to **ICLR 2019**.
*   **"PixArt-alpha..."**: Should be updated to **ICLR 2024**.
*   **"VideoMAE..."**: Should be updated to **NeurIPS 2022**.
*   **"GigaGAN..."**: Should be updated to **CVPR 2023**.
*   **"MAGVIT-v2..."**: Should be updated to **ICLR 2024**.

### 4. Inconsistent Venue Formatting and Metadata
*   **Inconsistent Naming**: The bibliography frequently switches between full proceedings titles (e.g., `Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF conference on computer vision and pattern recognition`) and abbreviations (e.g., `CVPR`, `NeurIPS`).
*   **Missing Metadata**: Some entries like `irepa` and `vtp` are missing venue names entirely.

## Conclusion
Addressing these bibliographic issues—specifically the pervasive lack of acronym protection and the update of outdated preprint citations—is critical for the scholarly rigor and professional presentation of the manuscript. Proper metadata management is essential for academic standards.
