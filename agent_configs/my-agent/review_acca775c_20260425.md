## Findings

I have performed a thorough review of the bibliography (`example_paper.bib`) for this submission. The reference list contains several technical issues that require attention to meet professional academic standards:

### 1. Missing Capitalization Protection for Acronyms and Proper Nouns
There is a pervasive lack of curly brace `{}` protection for technical acronyms and model names in titles. This will result in these terms being incorrectly rendered in lowercase due to ICML sentence-case enforcement. Examples include:
*   **Model Names**: `DeepSeek-V3`, `DeepSeekMoE`, `DeepSeekMath`, `Llama 2`, `Gemma 2`, `BLIP-2`, `LLaVA`, `GPT-4`, `FineWeb`, `DataComp-LM`, `OpenMoE 2`, `UniMoE-Audio`, `ReMoE`, `CoLT5`, `GShard`, `DeeBERT`, `ScatterMoE`, `AdaMoE`, `TC-MoE`, `XMoE`, `HMoE`, `ST-MoE`, `Lory`, `SeqTopK`, `Moonlight`.
*   **Technical Terms**: `ReLU`, `RMSNorm`, `RoPE`, `GQA`, `DiT`, `BERT`, `Adam`, `AdamW`, `ZeRO`, `muP`.

### 2. Outdated Publication Venues
Several key works are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been formally published in major venues:
*   **"Mixture-of-Depths..."** (`raposo2024mixture`): Should be updated to **ICML 2024**.
*   **"Scalable Diffusion Models with Transformers"** (`peebles2023dit`): Should be updated to **ICCV 2023**.

*   **"DeepSeek-V3 Technical Report"**: Now widely recognized as a formal technical release (2024/2025).

### 3. Inconsistent Venue Formatting and Metadata
*   **Inconsistent Naming**: The bibliography mixes abbreviated names (e.g., `NeurIPS`, `WWW`) with full conference titles (e.g., `Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems`).
*   **Potential Typos**: The author name "Smber" in `li2024dclm` appears to be a typographical error.

Addressing these bibliographic issues—specifically ensuring proper acronym protection and updating preprint citations—will significantly enhance the scholarly quality and professional presentation of the manuscript. Proper metadata management is essential for academic rigor.
