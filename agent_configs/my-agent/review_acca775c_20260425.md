# Citation Formatting Audit for Paper acca775c

**Paper ID:** acca775c-254b-410c-9252-c37ed998431f
**Audit Date:** 2026-04-25

## Summary
The bibliography for this submission contains several outdated citations for well-known works and exhibits inconsistent formatting and capitalization protection.

## Key Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several seminal works are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been formally published in major venues:
- **`cobbe2021training` (GSM8K)**: Cited as a 2021 preprint; this work is formally published and the citation should be updated to reflects its venue (e.g., NeurIPS).
- **`muennighoff2024olmoe`** and **`raposo2024mixture` (Mixture-of-Depths)**: These 2024 works have likely moved beyond preprint status or will soon; tracking their formal publication is recommended for a final version.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection
Technical acronyms and specific model names are frequently unprotected in the `title` fields, which will likely lead to incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles:
- Acronyms like **MoE**, **LLMs**, **SOTA**, and **GSM8K** should be enclosed in curly braces `{}`.
- Model names and specific techniques like **CoLT5**, **OLMoE**, and **DataComp-LM** also require protection.

### 3. Inconsistent Title Casing
The bibliography uses a mix of sentence-case and title-case for similar types of entries:
- `mixture-of-experts with expert choice routing` (sentence-case)
- `Mixture-of-Depths: Dynamically allocating compute...` (title-case for the first part)
- `Harder Tasks Need More Experts: Dynamic Routing in MoE Models` (title-case)

A unified casing strategy (typically sentence-case with protected acronyms for most ML venues) is recommended.

### 4. Author List Issues
The entry `li2024dclm` uses `and others` in the `author` field. While acceptable in some styles, for a primary reference like DataComp-LM, a more complete author list is often preferred in professional bibliographies.

## Conclusion
Updating the arXiv preprints to their formal publication venues and ensuring consistent capitalization protection for technical terms will significantly improve the scholarly rigor and professional presentation of the manuscript.
