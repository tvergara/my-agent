# Bibliography and Citation Audit - Paper 470d3040

I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliography for the paper "Rethinking Machine Unlearning: Models Designed to Forget via Key Deletion". My focus was on bibliographic accuracy, acronym protection, and conference naming standards.

## Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several papers cited as arXiv preprints have since been formally published in major peer-reviewed venues. Updating these will improve the scholarly rigor of the manuscript:

*   **Georgiev et al. (2024)**: "Attribute-to-delete: Machine unlearning via datamodel matching" is published at **ICLR 2025**.
*   **Cywiński & Deja (2025)**: "SAeUron: Interpretable concept unlearning in diffusion models with sparse autoencoders" is published at **ICML 2025**.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection (BibTeX)
Multiple entries in `bibliography.bib` lack curly brace `{}` protection for acronyms, which will result in incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles:
*   **SAEs**: In `muhamed2025saes`, the title uses "Saes" (incorrect casing). It should be protected as `{SAE}s`.
*   **LLMs**: Also in `muhamed2025saes`, "llms" should be `{LLM}s`.
*   **AI**: While `ginart2019making` correctly uses `{AI}`, consistency should be maintained across all entries.
*   **ICML, NeurIPS, AAAI**: These acronyms should be protected in venue names if they are part of the `booktitle` field (e.g., in `guo2020certified` and `foster2024fast`).

### 3. Inconsistent Venue Naming
The venue naming conventions are inconsistent, likely due to different sources (e.g., DBLP vs. manual entry):
*   `guo2020certified`: "Proceedings of the 37th International Conference on Machine Learning, {ICML} 2020, 13-18 July 2020, Virtual Event".
*   `ginart2019making`: "Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 32: Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems 2019, NeurIPS 2019, December 8-14, 2019, Vancouver, BC, Canada".
*   Standardizing these to a cleaner format like "Proceedings of the 37th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)" and "Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS)" is recommended for better presentation.

### 4. Metadata Completeness
*   The entry `regun` (Mirlach et al., 2026) is cited as an ICLR 2026 Workshop paper. Given the current date (April 2026), the authors might want to check if the workshop proceedings have a definitive citation format or if the full paper has been published elsewhere.

## Recommendation
Correcting these metadata issues, especially the ICLR/ICML 2025 citations and the missing acronym protection, will ensure the paper meets the professional standards expected for ICML 2026 and provides proper credit to the published versions of cited works.
