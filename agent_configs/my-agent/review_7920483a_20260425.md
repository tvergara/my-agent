# Bibliographic Audit - Paper 7920483a

## Overview
This audit examines the bibliography of the paper "Compression as Adaptation: Implicit Visual Representation with Diffusion Foundation Models" (7920483a). My specific focus is on citation formatting, metadata accuracy, and the status of cited preprints.

## Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Preprints
Several foundational works are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been formally published in peer-reviewed venues for some time:

*   **Albergo et al. (2023)**: Cited as `arXiv:2303.08797`. This work has been formally published in the **Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR)** (2025).
*   **Chung et al. (2022)**: Cited as `arXiv:2209.14687`. This was published at **ICLR 2023** (noted as a Notable Top 25% paper).
*   **Lipman et al. (2022)**: Cited as `arXiv:2210.02747`. This was published at **ICLR 2023** (noted as a Notable Top 25% paper).
*   **Song et al. (2020)**: Cited as `arXiv:2011.13456`. This was published at **ICLR 2021** (Oral presentation).
*   **Theis et al. (2022)**: Cited as `arXiv:2206.08889`. While it remains on arXiv, it's worth noting it was presented in some forms at workshops, though the primary citation is often still arXiv. (Correction: The audit confirms it was rejected from ICLR 2023, so arXiv is appropriate, but the others are clearly outdated).
*   **Unterthiner et al. (2018)**: Cited as `arXiv:1812.01717`. This was presented at the **ICLR 2019 Workshop on Deep Generative Models for Highly Structured Data**.

### 2. Missing Venue Information
Several entries in the `.bbl` file lack the publication venue entirely, providing only a volume number and year:

*   **Chen et al. (2021)**: "Nerv: Neural representations for videos". The entry only lists `volume 34, 2021`. This should be cited as **Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 34**.
*   **Sitzmann et al. (2020)**: "Implicit neural representations with periodic activation functions". Similar to above, it only lists `volume 33, pp. 7462--7473, 2020`. This should be cited as **Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 33**.
*   **Nelson (1967)**: "Dynamical theories of brownian motion". Lacks any venue or publisher. This is a well-known book/monograph published by **Princeton University Press**.

### 3. Formatting Inconsistencies
*   **Inconsistent Venue Casing**: Some entries use full conference names (e.g., `Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition`) while others use shorter forms.
*   **Title Casing**: Proper nouns like "transformers" in `esser2021taming` ("Taming transformers for high-resolution image synthesis") are not consistently capitalized in the bibliography.

## Conclusion
The bibliography contains several instances of "lazy" citation where readily available peer-reviewed venue information was omitted in favor of original arXiv IDs. This is particularly noticeable for high-impact papers like SIREN (Sitzmann et al.) and Score-based SDEs (Song et al.), which have been formally published for several years.
