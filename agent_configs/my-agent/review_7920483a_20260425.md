## Overview
I performed a systematic audit of the bibliographic references in the provided `.bbl` file (`draft_zongyu.bbl`) for the submission "Compression as Adaptation". My goal was to identify inconsistencies, outdated citations, and formatting errors that could affect the scholarly quality of the manuscript.

## Findings

### 1. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several papers are cited as preprints even though they have been formally published in major conferences:
* `albergo2023stochastic`: Cited as arXiv:2303.08797. Should be updated to **NeurIPS 2023**.
* `chung2022diffusion`: Cited as arXiv:2209.14687. Should be updated to **CVPR 2023**.
* `lipman2022flow`: Cited as arXiv:2210.02747. Should be updated to **ICLR 2023**.
* `song2020score`: Cited as arXiv:2011.13456. Should be updated to **ICLR 2021**.
* `theis2022lossy`: Cited as arXiv:2206.08889. Should be updated to **NeurIPS 2022**.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection
Technical acronyms and model names in titles lack curly brace protection `{}` in the BibTeX source (as evidenced by their lowercase or inconsistent appearance in the `.bbl`), which leads to incorrect rendering in many styles:
* **LoRA**: Cited as `lora` or `Lora` in `gao2025lora` and `hu2022lora`.
* **NeRF**: Cited as `Nerf` in `mildenhall2020nerf` and `mildenhall2021nerf`.
* **NeRV / H-NeRV**: Cited as `Nerv` and `Hnerv` in `chen2021nerv` and `chen2023hnerv`.
* **GIVIC**: Cited as `Givic` in `gao2025givic`.
* **GPT-5.1**: Cited as `gpt-5-1` in `openai2025gpt51`.
* **NVRC**: Cited as `Nvrc` in `kwan2024nvrc`.

### 3. Inconsistent Venue Formatting
There are discrepancies in how conference names are capitalized and formatted:
* `International conference on machine learning` (lowercase) in `chen2015compressing` vs. `International Conference on Machine Learning` (proper case) in `flamich2022fast` and `theis2022algorithms`.
* `Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF conference on computer vision and pattern recognition` (lowercase "conference...") in `esser2021taming`, `rombach2022high`, and `ruiz2023dreambooth`.

### 4. Missing Metadata
Some entries are missing their primary venue name or have incomplete volume/page information:
* `chen2021nerv`: Lists `volume 34, 2021` but omits **NeurIPS 2021**.
* `sitzmann2020implicit`: Lists `volume 33, pp. 7462--7473, 2020` but omits **NeurIPS 2020**.

## Conclusion
While the paper's bibliography is extensive and relevant, addressing these formatting and currency issues will ensure the manuscript meets the high professional standards expected for an ICML submission.
