# Bibliography and Citation Audit - Paper a99e0983

I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliography for the paper "Physics-Informed Policy Optimization via Analytic Dynamics Regularization". My focus was on bibliographic accuracy, acronym protection, and conference naming standards.

## Findings

### 1. Outdated and Inaccurate Citations
Several foundational papers in reinforcement learning are cited as preprints or with incorrect years/venues:
*   **Lillicrap et al. (2019)**: The paper "Continuous control with deep reinforcement learning" (DDPG) is cited as a 2019 preprint. It was formally published at **ICLR 2016**.
*   **Schulman et al. (2017)**: "Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms" (PPO) is an OpenAI technical report and arXiv preprint (2017). While technically correct, it is often cited as a technical report.
*   **Cranmer et al. (2020)**: "Lagrangian Neural Networks" was published in the **ICLR 2020 Workshop on Deep Differential Equations**.

### 2. Missing Capitalization Protection (BibTeX)
Multiple entries in `references.bib` lack curly brace `{}` protection for acronyms, which will result in incorrect lowercasing in many bibliography styles:
*   **Acronyms in Titles**: `PPO` (in `schulman2017ppo`), `SAC` (in `haarnoja2018sac`), `TD3` (in `fujimoto2018td3`), `MuJoCo` (in `todorov2012mujoco`), `PINN` (in `raissi2019pinn`, `liu2024pinnrobots`, `chen2022pinnrl`), `IQL` (in `kostrikov2022iql`), `CPO` (in `achiam2017cpo`), `HJ-BRL` (in `cheng2021hjbrl`).
*   **Software and Libraries**: `Gymnasium` (in `gymnasium`), `Stable-Baselines3` (in `sb3`).

### 3. Inconsistent Venue Naming
The naming of major conferences is inconsistent across the bibliography:
*   "Proceedings of the 35th International Conference on Machine Learning" vs "Proceedings of the 39th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)".
*   "Proceedings of the International Conference on Learning Representations" (missing the edition number, e.g., 7th, 10th).
*   "International Conference on Robotics and Automation" (should be formally "IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA)").

### 4. Technical Metadata
*   The entry `silver2018residual` is an arXiv preprint and remains so as of April 2026. However, its title "Residual Policy Learning" should have `{Residual Policy Learning}` or similar if the style requires Title Case.
*   The entry `banerjee2025pirlsurvey` lists authors as `Banerjee, C. and et al.`. The "and et al." is non-standard BibTeX; it should be `Banerjee, C. and others` to correctly trigger "et al." in the formatted output.

## Recommendation
Correcting these metadata issues, especially the DDPG citation and the missing acronym protection, will ensure the paper meets the professional standards expected for ICML 2026 and provides proper credit to the published versions of cited works.
