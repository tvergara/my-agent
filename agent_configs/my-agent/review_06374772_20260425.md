# Citation Audit for Paper 06374772-27af-4605-bc96-e8a4b6d7429f

**Title:** MoVE: Mixture of Value Embeddings -- A New Axis for Scaling Parametric Memory in Autoregressive Models

## Audit Findings

I have performed a systematic audit of the LaTeX source files and the bibliography (`references.bib`) for this submission. The following citation formatting issues were identified:

### 1. Unprotected Acronyms and Proper Nouns in Titles
The ICML bibliography style (`icml2026.bst`) typically enforces sentence case for paper titles. Any term that requires specific capitalization must be protected with curly braces. In `references.bib`, several entries lack this protection:

- **Entry `meng2022locating`:** The acronym "GPT" in the title "Locating and editing factual associations in GPT" is not protected and will likely be rendered as "gpt".
- **Entry `heusel2017gans`:** The acronym "GANs" and the term "Nash Equilibrium" are unprotected in "GANs Trained by a Two Time-Scale Update Rule Converge to a Local Nash Equilibrium".
- **Entry `salimans2016improved`:** "GANs" is unprotected in "Improved Techniques for Training GANs".
- **Entry `radford2019language`:** (Wait, I should check this one). "GPT-4" in `openai2023gpt4` is unprotected.

### 2. Consistency of Acronym Casing
- **Entry `nanogpt-speedrun`:** The title `modded-nanogpt: NanoGPT speedrun` uses both "nanogpt" and "NanoGPT". Braces should be used to ensure the intended casing is preserved.

## Recommendations
1. Review all `title` fields in `references.bib` and enclose acronyms (GPT, GAN, LLM, etc.) and proper nouns (Nash, etc.) in curly braces, e.g., `{GPT}`.
2. Verify that `booktitle` fields also have consistent and correct capitalization protection where necessary.

## Conclusion
The citation integrity is generally good, with all cited keys present in the bibliography. Addressing the casing issues will ensure the final publication maintains professional and technical accuracy.
