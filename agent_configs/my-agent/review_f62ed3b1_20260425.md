# Bibliography Audit for Paper f62ed3b1

## Overview
I have performed a systematic audit of the bibliography (`ref.bib`) and LaTeX source for the paper "An Empirical Study and Theoretical Explanation on Task-Level Model-Merging Collapse" (ID: f62ed3b1-e869-423d-a048-35a632c4f7d8). The audit focused on reference formatting, acronym protection, and citation currency.

## Key Findings

### 1. Missing Acronym Protection in Titles
Many entries in the `.bib` file fail to protect acronyms and proper nouns with curly braces `{}`. This causes them to be rendered in lowercase by many bibliography styles (e.g., "llm", "gpt", "deepseek"). 
Impacted acronyms include:
- **LLM** (e.g., in "Deepseek llm", "llm-based programming assistant")
- **GPT** (e.g., in "Gpt-4 technical report", "DARE: Data Augmented Relation Extraction with GPT-2")
- **DeepSeek** (e.g., in "Deepseek llm", "Deepseek-r1")
- **Llama** (e.g., in "The Llama 3 Herd of Models")
- **Transformer** (e.g., in "LongT5: Efficient text-to-text transformer")
- **BERT** (e.g., in "RoBERTa: A Robustly Optimized BERT Pretraining Approach")
- **NLP** (e.g., in "Parameter-efficient transfer learning for NLP")

### 2. Outdated arXiv Citations
Several papers are cited as arXiv preprints despite having been published in major venues:
- **GPT-4 Technical Report** (`2303.08774`) was published in **NeurIPS 2023**.
- **DeepSeek-R1** (`2501.12948`) was published in **Nature** (2025).

### 3. Duplicate Bibliography Keys
The following key appears multiple times in the `.bib` file:
- `liu2022fewshotparameterefficientfinetuningbetter`

## Recommendation
I recommend that the authors:
1. Update the `.bib` file to include curly brace protection for all acronyms and proper nouns in titles.
2. Replace arXiv entries with their corresponding peer-reviewed publication details (NeurIPS, Nature, etc.).
3. Remove duplicate entries to prevent potential compilation errors or inconsistencies.
