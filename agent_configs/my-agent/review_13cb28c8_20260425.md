# Bibliography Audit - Paper 13cb28c8

The following citation formatting issues were identified in the rendered bibliography (`icml_paper.bbl`):

## 1. Inconsistent Journal and Venue Casing
There is significant inconsistency in how journal names and conference venues are capitalized:
- Lowercase/Sentence Case: `Physical review letters` (Dax et al. 2021, 2023), `IEEE/ACM transactions on audio, speech, and language processing` (Hsu et al. 2021), `Advances in neural information processing systems` (Wu et al. 2021).
- Title Case: `The Astrophysical Journal Supplement Series` (Fei et al. 2024), `IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering` (Kemp et al. 2000), `Scientific Data` (Lamothe et al. 2025).

Standard academic practice requires consistent Title Case for journal and conference names.

## 2. Inconsistent Title Casing
Bibliography titles alternate between Sentence Case and Title Case:
- Sentence Case: `Intern-s1: A scientific multimodal foundation model` (Bai et al. 2025), `A decoder-only foundation model for time-series forecasting` (Das et al. 2024).
- Title Case: `Large Brain Model for Learning Generic Representations with Tremendous EEG Data in BCI` (Jiang et al. 2024), `Multi-Stage Learning for Radar Pulse Activity Segmentation` (Huang et al. 2024).

## 3. Potential Missing Capitalization in Acronyms
While many acronyms are correctly capitalized (e.g., `EEG`, `ASR`, `BCI`, `LLM`), some appear inconsistent:
- `Intern-s1`: Usually `S1` is capitalized in model identifiers.
- `scidiscovery`: The title `Scientific discovery in the age of artificial intelligence.` ends with a period inside the title field, which may lead to double periods in some styles.

## 4. Metadata Integrity
- `scidiscovery`: The title ends with a period which is unusual for a BibTeX title field.

Ensuring consistent casing and proper acronym protection across all entries will improve the scholarly presentation of the manuscript.
